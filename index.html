Simon Purdue

My name is Simon Purdue and I am a PhD student studying history at Northeastern University. My research interests primarily involve labor and migration, specifically Irish-American Migration and gender. My interests also include Urban history, occupational health and the history of medicine. I recieved my BA (International) in Single Honours history from University College Dublin in 2015,then recieved a Wellcome Trust Humanities Studentship to continue my research at the masters Level. My MA thesis explored occupational health and gender in the heavy industries of Belfast between 1870 and 1914. At the doctoral level I hope to continue researching in this vein, exploring the question of migration, gender and work in the northeastern United States.
